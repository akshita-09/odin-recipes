Dolma

Description

Dolma is one of the oldest national dishes of Azerbaijan.
The name of the dish, of Turkish origin, indicates the technique of its preparation - filling vegetable, grape, Rumex, hornbeam and quince leaves, or a thin dough layer, with various stuffings. In some regions it is called “sarma”.

Dolma, in Middle Eastern and Greek cuisine, any of various stuffed vegetable dishes—notably, the young leaves of the grapevine stuffed with a lemon-flavoured mixture of rice, onion, and, frequently, ground lamb. Although dolmas are usually eaten cold as an appetizer, Greek dolmades with lamb are served hot as a main course with an avgolémono sauce of egg yolks and lemon juice. Other vegetables such as zucchini, green peppers, cabbage, and onions are similarly prepared.

Even after being boiled and stored in oil for god knows how long, the grape leaves have a subtle but lingering wine-like taste that muddles into the dill and mint scrap up. Essentially, a dolma is a tiny flavor battlefield where different flavors keep coming out on top.

Ingredients

  • Cabbage cups, stems removed and blanched 8
  • Cabbage leaves as required
  • Chicken mince 250 grams
  • Rice soaked for 30 minutes ½ cup
  • Fresh mint leaves chopped 1 tablespoon
  • Fresh parsley chopped 1 tablespoon
  • Cumin powder ½ teaspoon
  • Tomato finely chopped 1 medium
  • Onion finely chopped 1 medium
  • Salt to taste
  • Lemon slices as required
  • Sriracha sauce for garnishing

Steps

  1. Heat some water in a deep non-stick pan. Place a bamboo steamer in it, cover and bring water to boil.
  2. Combine chicken mince, rice, mint leaves, parsley, cumin powder, tomato, onion and salt in a bowl and mix well.
  3. Take each cabbage cup, put some chicken mixture in the center and wrap it.
  4. Line the steamer with cabbage leaves, place the wraps over them. Place a lemon slice on top of each wrap, cover and steam for 20-25 minutes.
  5. Arrange the wrap on a serving plate, drizzle some sriracha sauce on top and serve immediately.
